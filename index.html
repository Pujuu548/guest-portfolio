Neeraj Chopra

Neeraj Chopra is an Indian track and field athlete and javelin thrower. He is the first Indian track and field athlete to win an Olympic gold medal. He is also the first Asian athlete to win an Olympic gold medal in javelin and the first Asian to win gold in his event at the World Championship

The turning point in Neeraj's life came when he decided to join the Athletics Academy in Panchkula. Under the guidance of experienced coaches, he honed his skills and gradually transformed from a promising talent to a force to be reckoned with. It wasn't long before he started breaking records at the national level, leaving spectators in awe of his raw talent and determination. But every hero's journey is marked by challenges, and Neeraj's path was no exception. In 2018, an unfortunate injury threatened to derail his dreams.

It was a setback that could have easily shattered the spirit of any athlete. However, Neeraj, fueled by an indomitable will, embraced the adversity and embarked on a journey of recovery that showcased his resilience. The year 2021 dawned as a pivotal chapter in Neeraj's story.
The Tokyo Olympics loomed on the horizon, and he was determined to make a mark. The world watched with bated breath as Neeraj Chopra stepped onto the grand stage, the Olympic Stadium, carrying the hopes of a nation. In a moment that would be etched in the annals of Indian sports history, Neeraj unleashed a throw that soared through the air with unparalleled grace and precision. The javelin pierced the Tokyo sky, carrying not just the dreams of a young man but the aspirations of a billion hearts. Neeraj Chopra had clinched the gold, becoming India's first-ever Olympic gold medalist in athletics.
